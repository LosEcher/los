## Design Context

### Users

LOS Web is primarily used by an advanced local operator who delegates engineering,
governance, research, and recurring project work to agents. The core job is to see
what needs attention, understand the evidence behind a run, intervene safely, and
decide whether work is verified and ready to close.

### Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and accountable. The interface should feel like a dependable local
operations console: compact enough for repeated use, explicit about state and risk,
and free of marketing language or decorative spectacle.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use a restrained industrial and utilitarian direction that extends the existing IBM
Plex typography, amber/green semantic accents, and light/dark themes. Prefer dense
lists, clear dividers, stable control dimensions, and evidence-focused layouts over
large cards, hero composition, gradients, glow, or playful illustration.

### Design Principles

1. Lead with the next operator action; keep raw runtime detail available through
   progressive disclosure.
2. Never collapse configured state, live state, verification, and external callback
   state into one label.
3. Preserve layout stability while runs stream, retry, or gain new evidence.
4. Keep critical actions keyboard accessible, explicit about their effect, and usable
   on narrow viewports without hiding core functionality.
